## LinkedIn Organic Content Strategy for B2B SaaS

## Project Overview

This repository contains research on LinkedIn Organic Content Strategy for B2B SaaS. The objective is to study how experienced practitioners use LinkedIn to build authority, engage audiences, and generate business opportunities through organic content.

## Why I Chose This Topic

I chose this topic because LinkedIn has become one of the most important platforms for B2B SaaS founders to build credibility, establish thought leadership, and generate demand through organic content. Understanding how experienced practitioners approach LinkedIn content can provide valuable insights into effective founder-led marketing strategies.

## Experts Selected

### Morgan J. Ingram
** RATIONALE FOR CHOOSING HIM: He views LinkedIn as a platform for building relationships that lead to business growth. His content focuses on social selling, trust-building, and meaningful engagement rather than aggressive sales tactics. This makes his approach highly relevant for B2B SaaS companies looking to generate organic leads and long-term customer relationships.

### Pierre Herubel
** RATIONALE FOR CHOOSING HIM: He teaches businesses to build authority by creating trust and credibility rather than optimizing for engagement. His emphasis on original insights and consistently solving customer problems makes his "Authority First" approach highly relevant to B2B SaaS content strategy.

### Sherry Shinde
** RATIONALE FOR CHOOSING HER: Her unconventional use of hooks that spark curiosity and encourage engagement. She also supports her insights with real client outcomes and case studies, making her content authentic and grounded in practical experience.

### Sakshi Tyagi
** RATIONALE FOR CHOOSING HER: She emphasizes mastering the fundamentals of LinkedIn growth through consistent posting and audience engagement. Her content demonstrates that sustainable growth comes from disciplined execution rather than shortcuts.

### Will McTighe
** RATIONALE FOR CHOOSING HIM: He views personal branding as a long-term business asset that shapes how founders are perceived by customers, employees, and investors. His practical use of AI to support consistent content creation also offers a valuable perspective.

### Fatima Khan
** RATIONALE FOR CHOOSING HER: She provides practical frameworks for building a personal brand on LinkedIn through audience-first content, strategic positioning, and structured content systems. Her emphasis on balancing visibility, trust, and conversion content makes her insights highly relevant for creating sustainable LinkedIn organic growth strategies for B2B SaaS founders and professionals.

### Jesse Chan
** RATIONALE FOR CHOOSING HER: She combines practical LinkedIn growth strategies with data-backed insights from her own experiments. Rather than relying on generic advice, she shares authentic, measurable observations—such as optimal posting times, content performance trends, engagement patterns, and the impact of links and formatting—making her content highly actionable for building an effective B2B SaaS LinkedIn strategy.

### Lara Acosta
** RATIONALE FOR CHOOSING HER: She demonstrates how authentic storytelling and strategic positioning help founders build a personal brand that attracts long-term business opportunities. Her focus is on creating credibility rather than chasing short-term engagement.

### Matt Barker
** RATIONALE FOR CHOOSING HIM: He shares firsthand experiences and real performance data from his own LinkedIn content, making his insights highly authentic and practical. Rather than relying on generic advice, he highlights overlooked nuances of LinkedIn organic growth—such as private engagement, audience behavior, content objectives, and the difference between vanity metrics and business outcomes. His evidence-based approach provides actionable lessons that are directly applicable to B2B SaaS content strategy.

### Amanda Natividad
** RATIONALE FOR CHOOSING HER: She advocates a value-first approach through zero-click marketing, where businesses educate audiences directly on LinkedIn before asking for anything in return. Her philosophy aligns well with how B2B SaaS buyers build trust before making purchasing decisions.

## Data Collected

- 10 B2B SaaS content experts
- LinkedIn profiles
- Recent LinkedIn posts
- YouTube video transcripts (where available)
- Expert annotations and rationale for selection
**Note:** YouTube transcripts were collected where relevant content was available. Some experts did not have YouTube channels, and others used their channels mainly for personal or non-professional content rather than publishing consistent educational material on LinkedIn strategy or B2B SaaS marketing. Therefore, transcripts were included only when they aligned with the research topic.

## Research Method

- Selected ten experts who actively create content on LinkedIn organic growth, B2B SaaS marketing, founder-led marketing, and personal branding.
- Manually collected and analyzed each expert's latest LinkedIn posts.
- Used Claude Code with the Supadata API and Python to automate the collection of YouTube transcripts where available.
- Documented expert rationales, platform links, and annotations in `sources.md`.
- Organized all research into structured Markdown files for easy reference.
