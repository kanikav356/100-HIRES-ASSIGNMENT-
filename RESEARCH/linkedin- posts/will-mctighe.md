** Original Post Content: 
It is STILL possible to grow on LinkedIn in 2026.
 If I was starting again, this is the system I’d use:

Most people skip straight to the writing. They open a blank page and try to think of something extremely profound.

But if you want to grow, most of the work needs to be done before you open that blank page.

Here's what to do:

1/ Write 3 different types of posts

Every post has one job. Growth posts reach new people through broad topics. Trust posts show how a person actually thinks and works. Sales posts use proof to give people a reason to buy.

Growth posts have the best shot at going viral, but the other two are what make the audience worth having.

2/ Identify your niche and stick to it

Pick a niche with valuable lessons to teach, then stay in that lane. Career development is a broad one that consistently does well.

Random, trend-driven posts might get a spike, but they attract followers who never come back.

3/ Create a content funnel

The mix matters as much as the posts. Aim for 4 growth posts, 2 trust posts, and 1 sales post each week.

Growth gets broader reach but rarely sticks. Trust makes a creator feel like a real person. Sales targets the followers who are already ready to buy.

4/ Choose proven post topics

There's no need to invent formats from scratch. Pick a trending topic or format in each category and adapt it to the niche.

Newsjacking, educational posts, objection posts and case studies are all easy to do on any topic.

5/ Nail every hook

The first 2 lines decide whether anyone reads the rest. I use 9 hook types, and the most reliable ones lead with real numbers, challenge what everyone believes, or say one thing whilst implying another.

I spend at LEAST half the writing time on the hook alone.

6/ Make your posts readable

Write the same way people speak, because no one wants to read an academic paper on LinkedIn. Follow a set structure based on the goal of your post. 

Avoid AI slop - people want to read YOUR thoughts, not Claude’s.

7/ Prioritize quality visuals

Any visual - infographic, carousel, video, selfie - can go viral. What matters most is that it’s high quality, striking enough to stop the scroll, and related to the content you’re posting about.

Growth on LinkedIn looks like luck from the outside.

In reality, it's seven decisions made before you press post.

** Original Post Content: 

10 rules for an iconic personal brand on LinkedIn:

When you’re starting out, it’s easy to try too much, too fast.

Try a new topic → write one post → it bombs → try a new topic, repeat.

Nothing stays around long enough for people to remember it.

But strong personal brands come from picking one thing, getting better at it, and saying it again and again. That's how you get remembered.

Here are the 10 rules I'd follow to build yours:

1/ 90:20 Rule

90% of your views come from 20% of your posts. Track your best performing hooks, formats, and topics every month, then do more of that.

2/ Repurpose your top posts

Every 3 months, repost your best content with recent updates.

I've turned infographics into carousels, changed the angle on old posts, and added new data and they perform all over again.

3/ Use visuals that hook

Use trending visual designs before they saturate. Large titles, contrasting colors, and unique designs stop the scroll.

My handwritten whiteboard infographics go viral a lot.

4/ Study the outliers

Find the posts in your niche performing 2x better than everything else. Study the hook, format, and angle that made them break out.

I use Saywhat Inspiration to find and analyze these posts.

5/ Hook with numbers

 Use real work, results, and lessons as proof. "I analyzed 390,000 posts" is more compelling than "Extensive research has found…"

But only add numbers when you can back them up!

6/ Use authority photos

Post photos that validate your authority - on stage speaking, with someone notable, at an exclusive event.

Whenever I have a speaking engagements or panel, I always make sure I get pictures for a future post.

7/ Solve one problem per post

Give each post one clear job: grow, build trust, or convert.

I plan my content as a mix of TOFU, MOFU, and BOFU - and never expect a post to hit all 3.

8/ Build a lead magnet

Solve one narrow, painful problem that your audience has with a checklist, a template, or an email course. Offer it for free in exchange for an email.

9/ Invest in your About section
Write about what you help people do. Keep it skimmable, use bullets, and end with a CTA like "Sign up for my newsletter" or "Book your first call here."

10/ Focus on one topic

Pick one specific problem for your audience, then keep coming back to it from different angles.

Iconic brands feel familiar. Pick what you want to be known for, then keep showing up with it.

** Annotation: Will presents ten practical principles for building a recognizable LinkedIn presence, including content repurposing, focusing on one core topic, analyzing high-performing posts, using strong visuals, and aligning content with different stages of the marketing funnel. The framework emphasizes consistency and strategic repetition as key drivers of long-term brand recognition.

** Original Post Content: 
If you think you've got nothing to post, I guarantee you're wrong. 
Everyone's sitting on a goldmine.

You have experience, perspective, and opinions that only YOU have. There's real potential there for great content.

But staring at a blank page, it feels impossible to know what to say.

So I laid out 10 types of posts for you to get started.

I broke them down with a real example for each, so you can use one and post it today: https://lnkd.in/gyFfySFX
Here they are. Choose where to start depending on your goal:

TOFU - Grow your audience

1/ Newsjacking Post
Borrow momentum from something people already care about. Make sure it’s current - interest can fade fast.

2/ Awareness Post
Reframe a common problem in a unique way. Make it broad enough to be applicable beyond your niche.

3/ Educational Post
Teach one useful idea your audience can try today. Ideally it’s something they can do in under 20 minutes.

4/ Spectacle Post
Challenge yourself to complete something with real stakes that people will want to watch. Make sure you follow through.
MOFU - Build trust

5/ Story Post
Share a real personal moment and the lesson it taught you. There has to be something actionable in it.

6/ Short Video Post
Show up on camera. Teach your audience something new or solve a problem step by step.

7/ Lead Magnet Post
Give away something useful in exchange for an email.
BOFU - Convert to clients

8/ Conversion Post
Show a real before-and-after transformation with actual numbers. The more specific details you have, the better.

9/ Objection Post
Answer the real reason people haven't bought yet…before they ask.

10/ Product Demo Post
Show your product solving a real problem in real time.

Next time you don’t think you have anything to say, check this list and just start writing.

** Annotation: Will categorizes LinkedIn posts according to the stages of the buyer journey—Top of Funnel (TOFU), Middle of Funnel (MOFU), and Bottom of Funnel (BOFU). Each content type serves a distinct purpose, from attracting new audiences through educational and newsjacking posts to building trust with stories and converting prospects through case studies and product demonstrations.
