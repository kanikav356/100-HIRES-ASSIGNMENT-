** Original Post Content: 
Corgi raised $378 million in 18 months, crossed a $2.6 billion valuation, and now insures more than 40,000 customers across 49 states.

Those are great numbers. But what stands out to me even more is that I cannot open LinkedIn these days without seeing Corgi somewhere on my feed.

They have made themselves impossible to miss, and here’s how they did it:

1. 3 magical words: Build in public.

Both co-founders, Nico Laqua and Emily Yuan, are constantly sharing what they are building and why. But it’s more than that. 

Brooke LeBlanc, their head of community, is one of the most fun people on my feed right now. Marie, Josh, Erika and Jamie put out really good content too. 

Across their whole team, everyone's personality comes through in their posts, and none of it is boring to read. (rare)

2. They turned physical infrastructure into the coolest marketing channel.

The 24/7 Corgi Café in the SF Financial District has become the unofficial clubhouse for founders pulling all-nighters, with perks for YC founders and a lot of their earliest customers coming out of the same batches. VCs now use the café to scout talent. Even Marc Andreessen has posted about them on X recently. 

By showing up for that community first, they have become THE YC insurance company.

3. Their brand identity is ridiculously cohesive.

Everything Corgi makes looks unmistakably like Corgi, down to the smallest detail. They have built a brand world and have kept every surface consistent across all of it. And their mascot, Trudy, is absurdlyyyy cute. (who does not love a cute doggo?)

It all comes down to a hundred small, specific, on-brand choices stacked on top of each other, until the whole company becomes impossible to ignore.

If you are a B2B founder trying to stand out, here is where I would start:

1. Get yourself posting first. 
2. Then bring in the rest of the team. This is where employee-generated content takes over and your brand starts showing up everywhere at once.
3. Build a community. Show up in comments, reply to people, give them a reason to gather around you.
4. Be a little creative. Find the one thing that makes people stop and remember you.

You will not get there in a week, but a few months of showing up like this will really change how people see you. 

Peace ✌ 

PS. Team Corgi, I have to ask. How are you all making posting look this effortless?

** Original Post Content: 
Too many people overcomplicate LinkedIn. 

Here's the 5-steps I use to book 5-6 meetings/ week. 

(Experts charge $1000 for this, save this for later 👀)

1. Fix your Positioning ( have 1 message )
2. Create content around this message 4-5 times a week
3. Share your work ( case studies ) 3-4 times a month
4. Engage with your ICP ( add meaningful comments )
5. Connect with and talk to 10-15 ICPs every single day

Do this, and promote your service shamelessly.

Talk about yourself, but about your audience too. 

Solve real people's problems. 
Engage with them, genuinely. 

This is mega-simple, but it works. Every time.

** Annotation: Sakshi outlines a straightforward five-step process for generating business opportunities through LinkedIn. ** Original Post Content: 
I am about to give you 35 post ideas. Save this if you're always "thinking about what to post."

To Build Connection -
1. How you got started (always works. should be everyone's 1st post.)
2. The reason you do what you do ( classic WHY post )
3. The 1st client you landed
4. A 4-years-ago vs now reflection (contrast is gold)
5. A post about your co-founder
6. The lowest moment of your founder journey so far
7. The exact moment you went all in & what all you gave up
8. #1 advice that changed how you run your business
9. A side of the founder life nobody talks about ( from your experience)
10. The dumbest mistake you made
11. The thing your team roasts you for (it humanises you.)
12. A post about a hobby completely unrelated to your work
13. A moment from this week, written the way you would text a friend

Visibility to reach a wider audience -

14. A take on the latest news in your category, posted within 12 hours of it breaking
15. Your honest prediction for your industry in the next 12 months 
16. The most overused phrase in your category and why it is now meaningless 
17. A teardown of a recent viral post or campaign in your space 
18. The thing every founder in your space is saying that is wrong 
19. A pattern you have spotted across 10+ sales calls in the last quarter 
20. The most expensive mistake you have ever made in your business, with the actual number attached
21. A "I changed my mind about X in the last 6 months" post, with the reasoning
22. A teardown of a recent failure in your industry, with your read on why it happened
23, The thing investors keep getting wrong about your category 
23. A "founders are about to learn this the hard way" warning, with a specific prediction

Credibility and conversion to make people trust you enough to take a sales call -

24. A case study, with the before, the work, the after, and the actual numbers 
25. The exact process you use for your most asked-about deliverable
26. A framework you have built that has your name on it 
27. The 5 most common objections from your sales calls, answered in full
28. A "what I learned from working with 50+ [type of client]" post 
29. The frameworks you have stopped using and exactly why
30. A client quote that says the thing you want your ICP to hear 
31. A "here is what working with us actually looks like, week by week" breakdown 
32. A "we are opening up [X] spots this quarter" post with a real deadline 
33. A "we said no to a prospect this week because of X" post 
34. The 3 questions every prospect should ask before hiring someone in your space 
35. A "DMs are open" post tied to one specific offer with one specific deadline 

That is 35 posts. You have ideas for the next 3 months. The only thing left to do is the part you have been avoiding.

Save and repost!
